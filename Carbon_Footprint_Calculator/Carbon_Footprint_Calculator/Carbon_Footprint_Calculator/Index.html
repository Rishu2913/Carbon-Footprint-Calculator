📘 About This Calculator

This Carbon Footprint Calculator estimates the amount of carbon dioxide (CO₂) emissions produced by daily activities. It uses information about transportation, electricity consumption, flights, and diet to estimate weekly carbon emissions. The goal is to help users understand how their lifestyle affects the environment.

📊 Understanding the Graphs

🚗 Carbon Emissions Breakdown

The bar chart shows emissions produced from driving, electricity, flights, and diet. It helps users identify which activity contributes the most to their carbon footprint.

⚡ Renewable vs Non-Renewable Energy

The pie chart shows the proportion of renewable and non-renewable electricity. Renewable energy includes solar, wind, and hydropower, which produce very low carbon emissions. Non-renewable energy comes from fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas.

🌍 Transport vs Lifestyle Impact

The line chart compares emissions from transportation (driving and flights) with lifestyle emissions such as electricity consumption and diet.

🌱 Renewable Energy

Renewable energy comes from natural sources that are continuously replenished such as sunlight, wind, water, and geothermal heat. These energy sources produce very little greenhouse gas emissions.

🏭 Non-Renewable Energy

Non-renewable energy comes from fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas. These resources release large amounts of carbon dioxide when burned and are a major contributor to climate change.
